Welcome to Nyx's Blog — a space dedicated to security research, ethical hacking, and the journey toward mastering penetration testing. This is where I document my path through the CEH certification and share practical insights from hands-on security work.

What You'll Find Here

This blog covers real-world security topics that matter:

  • CEH Preparation: Study notes, exam strategies, and certification insights
  • Penetration Testing: Lab setups, vulnerability discoveries, and exploitation techniques
  • Security Tools: Deep dives into Kali Linux utilities, Burp Suite, Metasploit, and more
  • Lab Documentation: Building and configuring test environments with Docker, VirtualBox, and vulnerable applications
  • Research & Analysis: Security vulnerabilities, attack vectors, and defense mechanisms

Why This Blog Exists

Security knowledge thrives when it's shared. This blog serves as both a learning journal and a resource for others pursuing ethical hacking. Each post represents hands-on experience, not just theory. Everything here is documented in a practical, implementable way.

The Nyx Aesthetic

You'll notice the classic retro terminal aesthetic — green phosphor screens, minimal design, pure content. Why? Because the early internet had it right. No tracking, no ads, no bloat. Just information, code, and ideas flowing freely across the network.

Getting Started

Explore the main page to browse all posts by category:

  • CEH: Certification-focused content
  • Pentest: Penetration testing techniques and findings
  • Tools: How to use security tools effectively
  • Lab Setup: Building your own testing environment

Let's Begin

Whether you're studying for CEH, building your first lab, or diving deep into security research — welcome aboard. The hacking community thrives on curiosity, persistence, and knowledge sharing. That's what this space is about.

Happy hacking. 🎛️
